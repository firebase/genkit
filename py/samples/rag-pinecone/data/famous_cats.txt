# Famous Cats Throughout History

## Internet Famous Cats

### Grumpy Cat (2012-2019)
Tardar Sauce, known as Grumpy Cat, became an internet sensation due to her permanently grumpy facial expression caused by feline dwarfism. She appeared in commercials, had her own movie, and her image was worth millions in merchandise.

### Lil Bub (2011-2019)
Lil Bub was a celebrity cat known for her unique appearance caused by genetic mutations. She had no teeth, extra toes, and a permanently protruding tongue. She raised over $700,000 for animals in need.

### Maru
A Scottish Fold from Japan, Maru is famous for his love of boxes and has been called the most watched cat on YouTube, with hundreds of millions of video views.

### Keyboard Cat (1984-2018)
Fatso, known as Keyboard Cat, became viral in 2007 when a video from 1984 showed him "playing" a keyboard. The meme is used to "play off" embarrassing moments.

## Historical Cats

### Félicette (1963)
Félicette was the first cat in space, launched by France on October 18, 1963. She reached an altitude of 157 km and survived the mission. A statue was unveiled in her honor in 2019.

### Unsinkable Sam (WWII)
A black and white cat who survived the sinking of three ships during World War II: the German battleship Bismarck, HMS Cossack, and HMS Ark Royal.

### Dewey Readmore Books (1987-2006)
Dewey was a library cat at the Spencer Public Library in Iowa. He was found in the book drop as a kitten and lived at the library for 19 years, becoming internationally famous.

### Trim (1799-1804)
Trim accompanied Matthew Flinders on his voyages to circumnavigate and map the Australian coastline. Flinders wrote a biographical tribute to Trim after the cat's death.

## Cats in Literature and Media

### Cheshire Cat
From Lewis Carroll's "Alice's Adventures in Wonderland," the Cheshire Cat is known for its distinctive grin and ability to disappear, leaving only its smile behind.

### Garfield
Created by Jim Davis in 1978, Garfield is an orange tabby known for his love of lasagna, hatred of Mondays, and lazy demeanor. The comic strip appears in over 2,500 newspapers.

### The Cat in the Hat
Dr. Seuss's iconic character, first appearing in 1957, is known for his red and white striped hat and mischievous adventures.

### Tom (Tom and Jerry)
The gray and white domestic cat has been chasing Jerry Mouse since 1940, making it one of the longest-running animated series.

## Scientific and Working Cats

### CC (Copy Cat)
CC was the first cloned cat, born on December 22, 2001, at Texas A&M University. She lived a normal life and had kittens naturally.

### Oscar the Therapy Cat
Oscar gained fame for his ability to predict the deaths of patients in a nursing home, curling up with them in their final hours. He was featured in the New England Journal of Medicine.

### Station Master Tama
A calico cat who became the station master of Kishi Station in Japan, helping revitalize the local economy and railway. She was appointed to the role in 2007 and drew tourists worldwide.
